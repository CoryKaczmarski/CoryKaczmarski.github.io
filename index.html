About Us

Black Sheep Egg Company is a farmer-owned, farmer-operated egg processing and packing company located in Northeast Arkansas. Our farms are scattered throughout the pasture belt running through Arkansas and Missouri. We are unique in that we process and package the eggs that are produced by the hens on our farms. We are composed of 8 farm families, and collectively, we have over 100 years experience in the poultry industry and almost 350 years experience in agriculture.

Our Values

  1. Farmers – Farmers are the heart of our operation, and we believe farmer input is critical and necessary for long-term success. Our company is both farmer-owned and farmer-managed to ensure a direct and integral connection to farmers.
  2. Family – Our farms are centered around the traditional farm family model in which all family members share in farm responsibilities and thus develop time-honored traits such as accountability, integrity, and dignity in work.
  3. The American Dream – We ascribe to the ideals of the American Dream, believing that all people should have equal opportunity to achieve success and prosperity through hard work, determination, and initiative.
  4. Equity in the Supply Chain – We believe that every link in the supply chain should have the opportunity to make a fair profit based upon the merits of each link’s contributions.
  5. Responsible Stewardship – We acknowledge that it is our responsibility to effectively care for the resources involved in our operations in a way that promotes good stewardship and sustainability.

Our Standards

Our hens live in cage-free barns with ample daily access to the outdoors. Although our standards in many ways mimic those set forth by various entities that provide certification for engaging in ethical and humane practices, we have deliberately chosen not to seek such certifications or make overt claims about the ethical nature of our standards. As farmers overseeing the care of our hens, we are whole-heartedly committed to doing what we believe is best for our flocks. The fact that each of our flocks are cage-free and free-range demonstrates our commitment to being good stewards. In addition, we are transparent with our standards, which allows our consumers to determine if these standards are sufficient. In addition, certifications are often costly, and these costs are ultimately passed on to the consumer. We believe such costs are unnecessary as long as we are clearly communicating our standards to those buying our eggs. In sum, we believe in and are committed to our production standards, and we trust consumers to determine if our standards meet their expectations for ethical and humane practices.
